Milled Mold

Rhino File

The goal of this assignment is to create a mold for a plaster casting. The steps include
first creating a mold from wax, to be used to create a mold from silicon, to be used for
creating the object of desire in plaster.

I looked for inspiration on Thingaverse. Thinking about the movement of the milling
bit, I searched for ideas that didn't include an overhanging ledge. I chose a pyramid
because of it's top down unfolding nature.

I borrowed the native STL file created by __________from thingaverse and opened it in Rhino,
where I had created a 3d mesh container that matched the size of a block of wax I intended
to mill.

In Rhino, I placed the pyramid inside my mesh container,in contact with its base.
As a 'whole', I exported the file as a (STl) file, one step toward manifesting the file
in G-Code, a language that the milling machine can interpret to carve my wax mold.


I opened my STL file in Fusion 360 where I was able to create and see or 'verify' a
milling tool path....But PIVOT. Nadia who was the makerspace saw my pyramid shape
and made the observaton that my pyramid had tiny detailing, to detailed for the
mill process to render.

I started over with looking for a simple iteration of a pyramid. I followed the
same steps to produce a milling pattern for the pyramid pictured above.

Once I opened the new pyramid in Fusion, I had an issue with the box that
represents my object area to be milled (I think): it hovered away from my box
I wasn't able to figure how to fix this, and was lucky to be at a makerspace where
with a student nearby who had a foundation of knowledgeable about Fusion , who was
very kind to help BUT even he became very frustrated. He believed the source of the
problem was the mesh nature of my stl from Rhino. TEXT

The solve for aligning the area with my file was...

I adjusted settings per instructions shared by Nadia,...

and created a tool path that in my limited experience appeared to look good!
I exported the G-Code. I attached my wax to the mill base using hot glue and hit start.

The above and below images of a successful milling operation on a small mill ARE DECEIVING.

• Back to Fusiong to try to override the error by adjusting retraction (only possible
with the help of Nadias
• After starting to mill and removing a lot of wax Nadia noticed it wasn't an appropriate tool path.
•I had to go back to Fusion and re-establish and verify a new tool path.
•Back in Bantam, the error message persisted
•Back to fusion where Cindy Feng made a more agressive adjustment to retraction.
•Back to Bantam, this time I hit the "start" button hoping to override the persisting error message.

Finally I was able to have progress milling.

Note! I had to hit the emergency stop when almost to the end, the mill bit ARM (not the mill bit)
made contact with my wax and began acting as a drill itself !!!(see the round cut out in my final mold).
Hence the "retraction" warning : My wax block was slightly too deep/thick, despite fitting under the mill bit.

I mixed up silicon at home and filled my mold.
